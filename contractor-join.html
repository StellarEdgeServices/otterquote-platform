Homeowner Cancellation Policy

Before you sign up, we want to be straight with you about one thing.

Homeowners on OtterQuote can switch to a different contractor in our network up to 3 days before their scheduled installation date. No questions asked.

We've all been there. Sometimes an employee drops the ball. Sometimes the homeowner changes their mind for no good reason at all. Whatever the case, it's going to happen occasionally — and we'd rather you know that upfront than be surprised by it.

Here's what that means for you:

  • OtterQuote handles the switch entirely. You don't make any calls, send any emails, or do anything.
  • Your platform fee is refunded in full. You're not out a dollar.
  • The homeowner selects another contractor in the network.

You'll lose a project here and there. You'll also pick up projects from homeowners who switched away from someone else. That's the deal. A platform where homeowners feel protected is a platform that keeps growing — and more homeowners on OtterQuote means more projects for every contractor in the network.

By checking the box below, you acknowledge that this cancellation right exists, that you're agreeing to it as a condition of working on the platform, and that you understand a platform fee refund is your sole remedy if a homeowner exercises it.

Tax Reporting Notice

OtterQuote pays contractors for project-based work facilitated through the platform. Because you are an independent contractor — not an employee — OtterQuote does not withhold federal, state, or local taxes from your fees.

Federal law requires us to report payments made to independent contractors. Specifically:

  • If you receive $600 or more from OtterQuote in a calendar year, we are required to file a Form 1099-NEC with the IRS reporting those payments. You will receive a copy no later than January 31 of the following year.
  • You are responsible for estimating and remitting all applicable federal income tax, self-employment tax, and state and local taxes on income earned through the platform.
  • OtterQuote may be required to collect your taxpayer identification information (W-9) before issuing payment. Failure to provide accurate information may result in backup withholding at the applicable IRS rate.

We recommend consulting a qualified tax professional regarding your obligations as an independent contractor.
