## Step 1 of 6 — Welcome to Otter Quotes

---

**Welcome to Otter Quotes.**

Here's how the platform works.

Otter Quotes delivers signed contracts to contractors — not estimates, not appointments, not leads. When a homeowner in your service area has an insurance claim or a retail exterior project that matches your trades, you see the opportunity, submit your bid, and we deliver you a fully executed contract.

The homeowner pays nothing. You pay a platform fee only when you win the job and the contract is signed.

This walkthrough shows you four sample opportunities — one insurance roofing claim, one retail siding project, one retail gutters job, and one multi-trade insurance claim covering both roofing and gutters. They're realistic but fictional. Take your time and explore each one.

## Step 2 of 6 — The Opportunity Card

---

Every opportunity starts with a card in your opportunities list. Here's what you'll see:

**Sample — Insurance Roofing (Fishers, IN)**

> **Roofing — Insurance RCV**  
> Fishers, IN 46037  
> Estimated value: $24,840  
> Material: Architectural — GAF Timberline HDZ  
> Posted 3 hours ago  
> *Open for bidding*

The card tells you what you need to decide whether to look closer: city, trade, funding type, material, and estimated value. For insurance jobs, estimated value comes from the homeowner's insurance estimate — it's what the carrier approved for the scope of work.

Click any card to see the full opportunity detail.

**What you'll find inside:**
- The full loss sheet summary — carrier, claim number, scope of loss, line-item breakdown, RCV and ACV figures, deductible
- Hover aerial measurement report with roof squares, pitch, ridge and valley lengths, and penetration count
- Homeowner notes (homeowner-written context that doesn't appear on the estimate)
- Photo gallery (field photos + aerial drone imagery)

Retail opportunities (siding and gutters) show the same detail view, with Hover measurements and the homeowner's material selection in place of a loss sheet.

## Step 3 of 6 — The Bid Form

---

When you're ready to bid, click **Place Bid**.

The bid form pulls in everything from the opportunity so you're not re-entering data you've already seen. For an insurance roofing job, you'll see:

- **Your contract price** — pre-filled to the RCV amount, editable
- **Shingle selection** — manufacturer, product line, and warranty tier
- **Value-adds** — each feature in your bid (ice and water shield, starter strip, ridge cap, felt paper, attic ventilation, etc.) marked as *included* or *available as upgrade with a price*
- **Bad decking contingency** — required on all roofing bids; enter your price per sheet and full re-deck price
- **Platform fee calculator** — as you enter your price, your net-to-contractor calculates in real time
- **Contractor message** — 3–5 sentences to the homeowner about why they should choose you

For retail siding and gutters, the form adapts to the trade — you'll see siding-specific warranty structures, material confirmation, and the prep/removal line items that matter for retail pricing.

When you submit, the homeowner sees your bid alongside any other contractors who have bid on the same opportunity. They compare and select.

*In this demo, the bid form is pre-filled for review. You can see every field — the walkthrough continues automatically.*

**Next: See your bid on the homeowner's comparison view →**

## Step 4 of 6 — The Homeowner's View

---

After you submit, the homeowner sees your bid on their comparison dashboard alongside other contractors.

Here's what they see about you:

- Your business name and years in business
- Your bid price and net out-of-pocket cost to them
- Your warranty (manufacturer + workmanship)
- Your materials selection
- Your value-adds marked as included or upgrade
- Your decking contingency price
- Your contractor message
- Your verified insurance and license badges

You're competing on quality and fit, not just price. On RCV insurance jobs especially, bid price is often identical across contractors because everyone bids the RCV amount — the homeowner chooses based on materials, warranty depth, and how much they trust the contractor's message.

When the homeowner selects you, you get an immediate notification. The platform then initiates contract signing.

**Next: The contract →**

## Step 5 of 6 — Signed Contract Delivered to You

---

Here's what makes Otter Quotes different from every other way to find jobs.

When a homeowner selects you, the platform takes your contract template — the one you uploaded in your profile — and auto-populates it with the homeowner's name, property address, and the bid details you submitted. The homeowner signs first. You counter-sign second.

What gets auto-populated for you:
- Homeowner name and property address
- Your bid price and payment terms
- Warranty terms from your bid
- Material selections and specifications
- Decking contingency language
- For retail jobs: the full Exhibit A scope-of-work document, line by line

You receive an executed contract, a copy of the insurance estimate (insurance jobs), the Hover measurement report, and the complete project confirmation. Everything you need to order materials and schedule the job is in one package.

The homeowner pays nothing. Your platform fee is a percentage of the contract price, charged when the contract is fully executed.

## Step 6 of 6 — Your Dashboard

---

Your contractor dashboard tracks everything in one place.

**Active opportunities** — jobs you can still bid on, with time remaining and your bid status  
**Your bids** — live bids with real-time status (under review, awarded, expired)  
**Signed contracts** — executed contracts with homeowner contact info, job details, and all documents  
**Payments** — your current balance, upcoming fees, and payment history  
**Profile** — your service area, contract templates, certifications, and auto-bid settings

Auto-bid is available for insurance roofing full-replacement jobs. When enabled, Otter Quotes submits your bid automatically the moment a qualifying opportunity is posted in your service area — at the RCV amount, with your saved value-adds and warranty settings. You review the match; the platform handles the speed.
